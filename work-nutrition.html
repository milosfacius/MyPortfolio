label redesign

label redesign · information design · iterations · visual design

Informative redesign of the American nutrition label.

challenge

The project is based around the idea of redesigning something that I identified as flawed, which in this case is the American nutrition label. Nutrition fact labels are clunky and hard to understand, leading most Americans to ignore them. Therefore I took upon the challenge to redesign the label - more specifically a Rigatoni Pasta box from Publix.

solution

The idea was to try and display the information in a balanced and simplyfied way, which would appeal to the consumers and simultaneously catch their attention.

I wanted to focus on whitespace with simple colors and also the soothing effect of a circle combined with informative bars, which as a result added stability and ease for the viewer and provided the consumer with the approriate amount of information, without being too busy and demanding.

iterations

The reason behind the selection of product to be redesigned, was that the information on the label was too confusing if not directly irrelevant for the consumer. The realization I had while trying to come up with, and sketch different design ideas was that the target audience differed in relation to the chosen direction I approached the redesign with. Therefore I wanted to identify some apparent flaws within the design in order to define how I wanted and didn’t want the information to be conveyed.

first iterations

The first iteration process was focused mainly around trying to come up with ideas on how to portray the nutrition facts in a new and optional way, without extracting the essential information from the original design.

At this point in the process my focus was to bring forth ideas in the rough rather than nitpicking our way to a final design.

second iterations

In the second iteration round I wanted to work with the feedback that I received. The following three themes were the main focuses:

color
hierarchy
whitespace

I also wanted to try and bring in a more clear focal point, to guide the user within the design. In the process i researched the specific demands from the FDA and tried to come up with a new way of portraying the serving sizes.

third iterations

The last iteration round was based upon the feedback that I received from both the first and the second round. I wanted to create a strong sense of hierarchy by taking advantage of whitespace.

The difficulties within this round were mainly that the amount of information I had to include, provided a difficulty in terms of the simplistic and calm designs that I wanted. I iterated on how I wanted the last design process to move forward and talked with colleagues about ideas that would in the end lead to the final design.

product

The final design draws inspiration mainly from the last iteration process. I wanted to use the whitespace layering that was used in the first concepts, and the color-scheme from the second one. The circle combined with the informative bars added a form of stability and provided the consumer with the appropriate amount of information, without being too busy and demanding.

The Vitamins & Minerals section was a combination of the hierarchy that resided within the second concept, while keeping the simplicity of the first. Even though this design might be difficult to implement in a real-life-setting, I still feel confident that the process and the overall effort put into it, showed some satisfying results. I learned how to use critique constructively to enhance my design, and the process provided me with the capability to show how very different design ideas, can be combined into one.
